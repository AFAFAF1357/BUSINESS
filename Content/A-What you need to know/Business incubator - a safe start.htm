Business incubator - a safe start

 

Before you decide to start your own business, you can test your business ideas in a safe environment.

Before you start running your own business activity, you might want to consider testing your business ideas and your entrepreneurial skills in a business incubator.

It is recommended, if you consider opening a business, but not have resources or knowledge, or simply are too uncertain to start a business on your own.

A business incubator is a convenient training ground, where a would-be businessperson can test their business ideas and see what it is like to run a firm.

How a business incubator operates

  • You sign an agreement with the incubator of your choice
  • Now you can operate as a virtual business
  • You are not a legal entity – your incubator is
  • Your incubator issues invoices for your products or services and signs contracts on your behalf.
  • You pay a fee for this services – fees vary, depending on the scope of the services offered, but usually they range from PLN 200 to PLN 500.

Who is a business incubator for

  • For anybody who plans to start their first business as a small or medium-sized enterprise or has an idea for business
  • Most often this solution is chosen by graduates and young people, but there are no age restrictions
  • The only condition is that it should be your first business
  • Please note that individual business incubators might have specific requirements – some specialise in specific sectors or industries, others are intended for graduates or post-graduate students only

When a business incubator is not a recommended solution

  • When you consider a large-scale or complex activity
  • When your business requires a special licence or a permit that are issued to individual entrepreneurs

What are the advantages

  • Professional assistance – you can consult an advisor who will assess your chances for success and help you choose your business strategy
  • Low costs – you only pay a monthly fee and do not need to worry about social security contributions, accounting, business consultancy, etc.
  • Access to facilities – you can use the incubator’s co-working space or rent a conference room for a meeting with an important client
  • Additional services – incubators offer various training sessions or can help you develop your business, promote your brand, acquire new clients or customers.

What are the disadvantages

  • Workflow is rather slow - it might take time to prepare or approve documents
  • Your business expenses are not tax-deductible
